After the Big Bang

    What happened?

    Let's fast forward to just after ...t = 0 seconds

    The Planck Era:
    Immediately after the Big Bang, and before the universe became just 10-43 seconds old, the Planck Era represented a time when the four fundamental forces were united.
    These forces are the electromagnetic force (governs behavior of lights and magnets), gravity (governs interactions between masses),
    the strong force (binds protons and neutrons together), and the weak force (important for nuclear reactions through fusion, and fission)
    In this tiny fraction of a second, temperature and pressure must have been above 10^32 Kelvin.
    The Grand Unified Theory (GUT) Era:
    Following the Planck Era, the GUT period is marked by two forces: first, gravity, and, second, the GUT force,
    which is the combined the electromagnetic, weak, and strong forces. If GUT theories are correct,
    rapid inflation in space occured as well as the separation of the strong and weak forces.
    There must have been a hypothetical X and Y Bosons allowing for proton decay. This period lasted for 10-43 to 10-38 seconds.
    Electroweak Era
    With gravity and the strong forces separated, the electromagnetic and weak forces were still together.
    Due to matter and antimatter being annihilated, all that exists is the byproducts: photons and pure energy.
    The temperature cools to 1015 Kelvin;
    By 10-10 seconds, the electroweak forces separate, causing the distinct four fundamental forces to be found.
    Particle Era
    Ordinary particles form
    Due to matter and antimatter being constantly created and annihilated, all that exists is the byproducts: photons and pure energy.
    The temperature cools to 1012 Kelvin, and here, particle creation and annihilation ceases.
    There are slightly more particles than antiparticles, allowing matter to be sustained and existence today.
    Nucleosynthesis era
    We are now at 0.001 second (1 millisecond) after the Big Bang
    Protons and neutrons spared from annihilation form heavier nuclei but are quickly annihilated by Gamma rays.
    The universe, however, cools until 109 Kelvin, in which case fusion of new nuclei ends.
    All that remains is a primordial mix of 75% Hydrogen and 25% Helium; this era ends after 5 minutes after the Big Bang.
    Era of Nuclei
    Hydrogen, helium, and free electrons compose the universe for the next 380,000 years as it expands and cools;
    it is a hot plasma that fails to form galaxies or other large structures.
    Protons and neutrons spared from annihilation form heavier nuclei. This process is called "recombination",
    whereby neutral hydrogen atoms are created. Reducing energy, these atoms reach their ground state by "decoupling" from their photons.
    Alongside this, the universe cools until 109 Kelvin, in which case fusion of new nuclei ends.
    The decoupled photons can be found as evidence in the Cosmic Microwave Background Radiation. For the first time, the universe is clear!
    Era of Atoms
    After the Era of Nuclei, differences in matter densities due to gravity formed halos, proto-galaxies, and other large scale structures.
    The first galaxies formed after 1,000,000 years after the Big Bang.
    Present day
    13.8 billion years later, we are voyagers on a beautiful planet sculpted from billions of years before us.
    There are many mysteries still unresolved: why is that there is a matter asymmetry?
    Why is the visible baryonic matter roughly 20% of all matter?
    Was dark matter created before the Big Bang?
